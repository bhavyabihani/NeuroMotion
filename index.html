About

Check about us

Neuro Motion is a cutting-edge exoskeleton hand designed to seamlessly integrate with the human nervous system. Our mission is to empower individuals with disabilities to regain hand functionality and independence. By combining engineering and neuroscience, this project aims to revolutionize assistive technology.

Project Overview

Neuro Motion is not just a device — it is a lifeline. By connecting directly to the nervous system, it translates neural signals into precise movements, enabling users to perform complex tasks effortlessly.

Potential Applications and Use Cases

  • Daily Living: Performing everyday tasks such as eating, dressing, and grooming can be facilitated by the exoskeleton.
  • Work and Employment: Individuals with arm and hand disabilities can potentially participate in a wider range of occupations
  • Recreation and Hobbies: The exoskeleton can empower users to engage in leisure activities such as playing sports or pursuing artistic hobbies

We believe Neuro Motion is the beginning of a revolution in assistive devices. Your support and feedback are invaluable as we refine our work. Let’s redefine possibilities together!

Working

CHECK HOW THE HAND WORKS

Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) Technology

The NeuroMotion exoskeleton hand operates using advanced brain-computer interface (BCI) technology, which establishes a direct communication link between the user’s brain and the exoskeleton device. Our system utilizes non-invasive electrodes that are applied to the skin, capturing brain wave activity to interpret movement intentions in real-time.

Signal Detection and Processing

Once the electrodes detect electrical activity in the brain, the signals are transmitted to a processing unit where sophisticated algorithms analyze and interpret the data. These algorithms translate the interpreted signals into precise commands that control the movements of the exoskeleton hand, enabling actions such as grasping, releasing, and manipulating objects.

Brain Waves

NeuroMotion's exoskeleton hand detects specific brain wave patterns through non-invasive electrodes applied to the skin. By analyzing these brain wave patterns, NeuroMotion's exoskeleton hand can accurately interpret the user's intentions and translate them into corresponding movements of the exoskeleton device, allowing for intuitive and natural control.

Natural Movement

The design of the NeuroMotion hand allows for a range of movements that mimic natural hand function, providing users with a more intuitive experience. This enables them to perform complex tasks, such as holding a cup, or picking up small objects, with ease and confidence.
