About

Check about us

Welcome to NeuroMotion, where innovation meets compassion. We are a pioneering company dedicated to transforming the lives of individuals with limb loss through our groundbreaking brain-controlled prosthetic hand. Our mission is to empower users by providing advanced technology that seamlessly connects their thoughts to movement, enabling them to regain independence and functionality.

Our Vision

At NeuroMotion, we envision a world where everyone has access to advanced prosthetic solutions that are not only functional but also affordable. We believe in harnessing the power of technology to create devices that adapt to the unique needs of each user, fostering a sense of normalcy and empowerment.

Our Technology

Our prosthetic hand is at the forefront of innovation, utilizing state-of-the-art brain-computer interface (BCI) technology. Here are some key features of our product:

  • Brain Wave Control: Users can control the prosthetic hand using their brain waves, allowing for intuitive and natural movement.
  • Non-Invasive Sensors: Our technology employs non-invasive sensors, eliminating the need for risky surgeries and making our solution accessible to a broader audience.

Working

CHECK HOW THE HAND WORKS

Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) Technology

The NeuroMotion prosthetic hand operates using advanced brain-computer interface (BCI) technology, which establishes a direct communication link between the userâ€™s brain and the prosthetic device. Our system utilizes non-invasive electrodes that are applied to the skin, capturing brain wave activity to interpret movement intentions in real-time.

Signal Detection and Processing

Once the electrodes detect electrical activity in the brain, the signals are transmitted to a processing unit where sophisticated algorithms analyze and interpret the data. These algorithms translate the interpreted signals into precise commands that control the movements of the prosthetic hand, enabling actions such as grasping, releasing, and manipulating objects.

Brain Waves

NeuroMotion's prosthetic hand detects specific brain wave patterns through non-invasive electrodes applied to the skin. By analyzing these brain wave patterns, NeuroMotion's prosthetic hand can accurately interpret the user's intentions and translate them into corresponding movements of the prosthetic device, allowing for intuitive and natural control.

Natural Movement

The design of the NeuroMotion hand allows for a range of movements that mimic natural hand function, providing users with a more intuitive experience. This enables them to perform complex tasks, such as holding a cup, or picking up small objects, with ease and confidence.
